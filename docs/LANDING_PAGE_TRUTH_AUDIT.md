## 2. Why each claim was risky

The previous public landing page implied a mature enterprise platform with customer proof, technical guarantees, and regulated pharmacy readiness. Those claims were risky because the current verified product state is beta-stage, with Restaurant as the most developed workflow and Retail / Commerce, Pharmacy, and Services / Projects still in active development or future beta scope.

Performance claims such as "sub-100ms" and broad security claims such as "end-to-end encryption for every transaction" require technical validation before being marketed. Compliance, prescription, patient history, and modern clinic language could imply regulated healthcare readiness that Q360 should not claim publicly.

Named testimonials, star ratings, "500+ enterprises", and "50 locations" style claims require real customer evidence and review provenance. They were removed because they were not verified.

## 3. What is safe to claim today

Q360 can safely be described as a modular operations workspace for businesses.

Safe current claims:

- Q360 helps businesses organize customers, work, orders, invoices, payment follow-up, and team workflows in one place.
- Restaurant operations are currently the most developed Q360 workflow.
- Retail and Commerce are in active development.
- Pharmacy is a future beta direction for medicine retail operations.
- Services and Projects are active development / beta scope.
- Current foundations include authentication, onboarding, role-based access, tenant / business identity, audit foundations, operational workflows, dashboard structure, and feature toggles where implemented.
- Real-time sync and offline capability are planned.

## 4. What must wait for customer evidence

These claims should wait until Q360 has verifiable customer proof:

- Customer counts, company counts, location counts, or adoption scale.
- Testimonials, star ratings, named customer quotes, and logos.
- Measured customer outcomes such as faster growth, reduced cost, added locations, or improved revenue.
- "Trusted by", "leading", "best", "#1", or similar market leadership language.

## 5. What must wait for technical validation

These claims should wait for formal technical validation and documentation:

- Specific latency guarantees such as "sub-100ms".
- End-to-end encryption claims for transactions or sensitive records.
- Security labels such as bank-grade, military-grade, vault security, or fully secure.
- Compliance readiness or regulatory guarantees.
- Real-time sync or offline-first production capability.
- Autonomous AI action, decision-making, or unsupervised workflow execution.

## 6. Q AI positioning rules

Public name: Q.

Approved positioning:

- "Q is your AI operations teammate inside Q360."
- Q provides suggestions, summaries, alerts, and drafts.
- Q helps owners understand what needs attention next using business activity and data recorded in Q360.
- Sensitive or important actions require owner approval.
- Q is available for selected beta workflows.

Do not call Q an AI employee. Do not claim Q runs the business, replaces the team, makes independent decisions, or makes financial, legal, medical, employment, or compliance decisions.
